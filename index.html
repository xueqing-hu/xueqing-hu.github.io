I am a PhD researcher at Ghent University, Belgium. I work on quantitative assessment of biological deterioration on historic building façades, with a focus on connecting image-based detection, material properties, and hygrothermal behaviour.

Education

  • PhD, Ghent University, Ghent, Belgium
    Building physics / heritage façade deterioration
  • MSc, [Southeast university], Nanjing, China
    Heritage science / building materials / spectral analysis

Research

My research uses hyperspectral imaging, RGB imaging, interpretable machine learning, and hygrothermal modelling to study bio-colonisation and moisture-related deterioration on heritage surfaces.

The broader goal is to develop robust and practical workflows for detecting façade bio-colonisation, interpreting its environmental and material drivers, and supporting long-term condition assessment of historic buildings.
